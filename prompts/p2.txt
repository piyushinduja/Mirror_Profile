## ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORK

### THE THREE LAYERS OF IDENTITY

**Layer 1: PUBLIC PERSONA**
- How does the subject present themselves to the world?
- What identity do they project in professional/social contexts?
- What do they want others to believe about them?
- What traits do they emphasize when describing themselves to others?

**Layer 2: PRIVATE SELF-VIEW**
- How does the subject see themselves when alone?
- What is their internal narrative about who they are?
- What do they believe their "true self" to be?
- Where is there vulnerability, doubt, or complexity they don't show publicly?

**Layer 3: SHADOW IDENTITY**
- What traits do they disown or refuse to acknowledge in themselves?
- What traits do they criticize harshly in others (potential projections)?
- Where do their stated self-view and behavioral evidence contradict?
- What aspects of themselves are they defending against or avoiding?

### IDENTITY COHERENCE vs. FRAGMENTATION

**Coherence Indicators:**
- Consistency across contexts
- Integrated self-narrative
- Acknowledgment of contradictions
- Comfort with complexity
- Alignment between values and behavior

**Fragmentation Indicators:**
- Different "selves" in different contexts
- Unacknowledged contradictions
- Rigid self-definitions
- Defensive about inconsistencies
- Gap between ideal self and actual self

## WRITING REQUIREMENTS

### AUDIENCE & VOICE
- **Write directly to the subject using second person ("you," "your")**
- This is THEIR mirror profile—they are reading about themselves
- Example: "You present yourself as...", "Your public persona emphasizes...", "You don't fully acknowledge..."
- Make it feel like a personal letter of deep insight written specifically for them

### DEPTH & NUANCE
- Go beyond surface observations
- Explore psychological mechanisms
- Identify patterns across multiple answers
- Acknowledge ambiguity and complexity
- Avoid reductionist labels

### EVIDENCE-BASED
- Quote directly from their answers frequently (use quotation marks with no question reference)
- **DO NOT include question references like (Q21) or (Q31)**
- Weave quotes naturally into sentences: You described yourself as "someone who..."
- Cite behavioral evidence, not just stated beliefs
- Point out contradictions with specific examples from their own words

### TONE
- Professional but warm and personal
- Compassionate but honest
- Analytical but not cold
- Direct about shadow material without being judgmental
- Like a trusted advisor who sees them clearly and wants their growth

### AUTHENTICITY
- Use their own words and phrases throughout
- Preserve their linguistic style when quoting
- Don't sanitize or over-intellectualize
- Let their authentic voice come through in the quotes you select

### INTEGRATION
- Connect to other sections when relevant (preview connections)
- Show how their identity architecture affects other areas of their psychology
- Build narrative threads that will continue through the profile

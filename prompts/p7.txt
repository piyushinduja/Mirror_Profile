Identify the subject's major psychological and environmental stressors, detail their coping mechanisms (healthy and unhealthy), analyze their long-term resilience strategies, assess recovery speed from setbacks, and identify resilience resources (social support, self-care, reframing).

## ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORK

### PRIMARY STRESSORS

**Environmental Stressors:**
- Work conditions (chaos, micromanagement, incompetence, politics)
- People types (lazy, negative, dishonest, aggressive)
- Physical environment (mess, noise, constraint)
- Time pressure and deadlines

**Psychological Stressors:**
- Loss of control or autonomy
- Uncertainty and ambiguity
- Performance pressure and expectations
- Fear of failure or judgment
- Feeling stuck or stagnant
- Conflict and confrontation

**Existential Stressors:**
- Meaninglessness or purposelessness
- Not making an impact
- Wasting time on trivial matters
- Not living up to potential

### STRESS RESPONSE PATTERN

**Immediate Reaction (First Wave):**
- Physical: tension, activation, shutdown, fatigue
- Emotional: anger, anxiety, overwhelm, numbness
- Cognitive: racing thoughts, clarity, paralysis, tunnel vision
- Behavioral: fight, flight, freeze, fawn

**Secondary Response (Processing):**
- How they make sense of the stressor
- Internal dialogue and narrative
- Who/what they blame
- Meaning they assign

### COPING MECHANISMS

**Healthy Coping:**
- Physical: exercise, walking, breathing, rest
- Social: talking it out, seeking counsel, connection
- Cognitive: reframing, perspective-taking, problem-solving
- Spiritual: meditation, prayer, nature, surrender
- Creative: expression, creation, movement

**Unhealthy Coping:**
- Avoidance: procrastination, distraction, numbing
- Aggression: lashing out, blaming, controlling
- Rumination: obsessive thinking, catastrophizing
- Substances: alcohol, drugs, excessive stimulants
- Workaholism: escape through overwork

**Coping Effectiveness:**
- Which strategies actually reduce stress?
- Which provide temporary relief but create more problems?
- Awareness of coping patterns?

### RECOVERY & RESILIENCE

**Recovery Speed:**
- How quickly do they bounce back from setbacks?
- What helps them recover faster?
- What slows recovery or keeps them stuck?

**Resilience Resources:**
- Social support systems (who they turn to)
- Self-care practices (what restores them)
- Psychological resources (optimism, self-compassion, faith)
- Environmental resources (nature, space, retreat)

**Resilience Strategies:**
- Acceptance and letting go
- Reframing and meaning-making
- Learning and growth mindset
- Compartmentalization and perspective
- Humor and lightness

### STRESS TOLERANCE & BURNOUT RISK

**Stress Tolerance:**
- How much stress can they handle before breaking?
- Warning signs of overwhelm
- Chronic stress indicators

**Burnout Assessment:**
- Physical exhaustion patterns
- Emotional depletion indicators
- Cynicism and detachment
- Reduced efficacy feelings
- Current burnout level

### STRESS & PERFORMANCE RELATIONSHIP

**Optimal Stress (Eustress):**
- What level of challenge brings out their best?
- When does stress enhance performance?

**Distress Threshold:**
- When does stress impair performance?
- Tipping point from productive to destructive stress

## WRITING REQUIREMENTS

### AUDIENCE & VOICE
- **Write directly to the subject using second person ("you," "your")**
- This is THEIR mirror profile—they are reading about themselves
- Example: "Your primary stressors are...", "When stressed, you...", "Your resilience comes from..."
- Make it feel like someone is explaining their stress blueprint to them

### DEPTH & NUANCE
- Distinguish between healthy and unhealthy coping honestly
- Show the effectiveness gap (what they do vs. what actually works)
- Identify patterns they may not consciously recognize
- Assess burnout risk truthfully without catastrophizing

### EVIDENCE-BASED
- Quote directly from their answers frequently (use quotation marks with no question reference)
- **DO NOT include question references like (Q12) or (Q26)**
- Weave quotes naturally: When describing how you handle stress, you said "..."
- Use their specific challenge/setback story as primary evidence
- Point to behavioral patterns across multiple stress examples

### TONE
- Validating of their stress experiences
- Compassionate about coping struggles
- Honest about unhealthy patterns without shame
- Encouraging about resilience capacity
- Like a trauma-informed therapist who understands stress physiology

### AUTHENTICITY
- Use their own language about stress and coping
- Preserve their emotional intensity when describing stressors
- Don't minimize what stresses them
- Let their resilience and struggles both show

### INTEGRATION
- Connect stressors to values violations (Section 2)
- Link coping to emotional regulation (Section 4)
- Show how stress affects decision-making (Section 3)
- Preview energy management (Section 10)

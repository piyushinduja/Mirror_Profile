## ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORK

### GOAL INVENTORY & CATEGORIZATION

**Stated Goals by Domain:**
- **Legacy Goals**: What they want to leave behind, impact on the world
- **Financial Goals**: Wealth, income, financial security, abundance
- **Career/Professional Goals**: Business success, position, influence, creation
- **Health/Physical Goals**: Fitness, energy, longevity, physical capability
- **Relationship Goals**: Partnership, family, friendship, connection quality
- **Personal Growth Goals**: Learning, skills, self-improvement, development
- **Spiritual/Meaning Goals**: Purpose, transcendence, connection to something greater
- **Lifestyle Goals**: Freedom, adventure, experiences, quality of life

**Goal Hierarchy:**
- Which goals get the most energy and attention?
- Which goals are stated but not actively pursued?
- What does actual behavior reveal about priority?

### GOAL-SETTING PROCESS

**How Goals Are Formed:**
- Intuitive/feeling-based selection
- Analytical/strategic planning
- External expectation vs. internal desire
- Response to pain/problem vs. attraction to vision

**Goal Characteristics:**
- Specific and measurable vs. vague and aspirational
- Time-bound with deadlines vs. open-ended
- Realistic and achievable vs. stretch and ambitious
- Process-oriented vs. outcome-oriented

**Goal Review & Adjustment:**
- How often do they revisit goals?
- Flexibility to adjust vs. rigid adherence
- Learning from goal failure

### MOTIVATIONAL DRIVE TYPE

**Approach Motivation (Moving TOWARD):**
- Pursuing positive outcomes and visions
- Attraction to possibility and growth
- Energized by what could be
- Optimistic and expansive orientation

**Avoidance Motivation (Moving AWAY FROM):**
- Escaping negative outcomes and pain
- Protection from failure, loss, or discomfort
- Energized by problem-solving and preventing disaster
- Vigilant and defensive orientation

**Mixed Motivation:**
- Combination of both with analysis of which dominates
- When each type activates
- Sustainability of each motivation type

### ACHIEVEMENT DRIVERS

**Core Achievement Needs:**
- Accomplishment and mastery
- Recognition and status
- Impact and contribution
- Autonomy and freedom
- Creativity and self-expression
- Competition and winning

**What Sustains Action:**
- Intrinsic satisfaction vs. external rewards
- Progress indicators they need
- How they maintain momentum
- What causes them to quit or persist

### GOAL-RELATED COGNITIVE BIASES

**Planning Fallacy:**
- Underestimating time, resources, or difficulty required
- Optimism bias in project timelines
- Evidence from their stated goals and past experience

**Sunk Cost Fallacy:**
- Continuing pursuit due to past investment rather than future value
- Difficulty abandoning goals even when no longer aligned

**Goal Dilution:**
- Too many simultaneous goals reducing effectiveness
- Scattered focus vs. concentrated effort

**Perfectionism Paralysis:**
- Goals that never launch due to perfectionist standards
- Analysis paralysis preventing action

### GOAL COHERENCE & CONFLICT

**Value Alignment:**
- Do goals align with stated values?
- Hidden value conflicts in goal selection
- Goals that serve ego vs. authentic self

**Goal Conflicts:**
- Competing goals that can't coexist
- Time/energy conflicts between goal domains
- Which goals sacrifice which

### EXECUTION PATTERNS

**Initiation:**
- Fast starter vs. slow to begin
- Planning phase length
- Over-preparation vs. under-preparation

**Persistence:**
- Follow-through consistency
- Reaction to obstacles
- Grit and determination level

**Completion:**
- Finishing rate
- What prevents completion
- Celebration of achievement or immediate next goal

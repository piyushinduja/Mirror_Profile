### RHETORICAL STYLE

**Core Communication Style:**
- **Direct**: Blunt, clear, no sugar-coating
- **Diplomatic**: Tactful, measured, considerate
- **Analytical**: Data-driven, logical, structured
- **Emotional**: Feeling-based, expressive, vulnerable
- **Storytelling**: Narrative, anecdotal, experiential
- **Questioning**: Socratic, curious, probing

**Directness Level:**
- How straightforward are they?
- Do they soften messages or deliver them raw?
- Cultural/situational variation in directness

**Assertiveness:**
- Passive, assertive, or aggressive communication
- How they express needs and boundaries
- Comfort with saying no

### LINGUISTIC PATTERNS - CRITICAL SECTION

**Vocabulary Patterns:**
- Frequently used words and phrases (extract specific recurring language)
- Industry/domain-specific terminology
- Colloquialisms, slang, or formal language
- Emotional vocabulary richness
- Abstract vs. concrete language preference

**Sentence Structure:**
- Short and punchy vs. long and complex
- Complete sentences vs. fragments
- Organized and linear vs. associative and tangential
- Use of lists, bullets, sequential thinking

**Tone Characteristics:**
- Intensity level (calm, moderate, intense, explosive)
- Enthusiasm and energy
- Confidence vs. hesitation
- Warmth vs. coolness
- Humor presence and type

**Speech Patterns:**
- Pacing (rapid, moderate, slow)
- Rhythm and flow (smooth, choppy, rambling)
- Self-interruption and self-correction
- Thinking out loud vs. pre-formulated thoughts
- Qualifiers and hedging ("I think," "maybe," "kind of")

**Emotional Expression Style:**
- How emotions show up in language
- Passionate intensity vs. measured control
- Vulnerability and openness in expression
- When they become most animated

**Metaphors & Conceptual Frameworks:**
- Recurring metaphors or imagery
- Conceptual language patterns (business, sports, spiritual, military, etc.)
- How they make abstract concepts concrete

## EXTRACTION REQUIREMENTS

For each answer provided, you must extract and document:

### 1. BEHAVIORAL INDICATORS
- Concrete examples and specific behaviors mentioned
- Patterns that repeat across multiple answers
- Contradictions or inconsistencies between answers
- Behavioral evidence (what they DO vs. what they SAY)

### 2. LINGUISTIC ANALYSIS
- **Vocabulary patterns**: What words/phrases do they use repeatedly?
- **Sentence structure**: Short/punchy vs. long/complex? Organized vs. stream-of-consciousness?
- **Tone markers**: Intensity, confidence, hesitation, passion, defensiveness
- **Speech patterns**: Do they ramble? Are they concise? Do they self-correct?
- **Emotional language**: What emotions are expressed and how frequently?
- **Metaphors and imagery**: What conceptual frameworks do they use?
- **Qualifiers and hedging**: "I think," "maybe," "probably," "definitely," "always"
- **Self-reference patterns**: First person usage, self-awareness markers

### 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL MARKERS
- **Identity statements**: Who they say they are, who they want to be, who they fear being
- **Value declarations**: What they explicitly state matters to them
- **Revealed values**: What their behaviors/choices actually demonstrate they value
- **Value conflicts**: Where stated values contradict behavioral evidence
- **Core fears**: What do they avoid, worry about, protect against?
- **Core desires**: What do they move toward, dream about, prioritize?
- **Defense mechanisms**: Rationalization, projection, denial, intellectualization
- **Shadow material**: Traits they disown in themselves but criticize in others

### 4. RELATIONAL PATTERNS
- How they describe interactions with others
- Power dynamics (dominance, submission, equality-seeking)
- Attachment indicators (secure, anxious, avoidant)
- Conflict resolution patterns
- Boundary management (rigid, flexible, porous)

### 5. COGNITIVE PATTERNS
- How they describe their thinking process
- Decision-making steps they mention
- Problem-solving approaches
- Learning style preferences
- Information processing (analytical, intuitive, somatic)
- Cognitive biases visible in their reasoning

### 6. EMOTIONAL PATTERNS
- Emotional self-awareness level
- Emotional regulation strategies
- What triggers emotional responses
- Emotional vocabulary sophistication
- Comfort with vulnerability

### 7. MOTIVATIONAL ARCHITECTURE
- What energizes them
- What drains them
- Intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation balance
- Approach motivation (moving toward) vs. avoidance motivation (moving away from)
- Achievement, affiliation, or power motivation dominance

### 8. TEMPORAL PATTERNS
- Past orientation (nostalgia, regret, learning)
- Present orientation (mindfulness, immediacy)
- Future orientation (planning, vision, anxiety)
- Time management and urgency relationship

### 9. STRESS & RESILIENCE INDICATORS
- Stressors they identify
- Coping mechanisms (healthy and unhealthy)
- Recovery speed from setbacks
- Resilience resources (social support, self-care, reframing)

### 10. ARCHETYPAL INDICATORS
- Roles they naturally adopt (Leader, Creator, Rebel, Healer, Warrior, Sage, etc.)
- Hero's journey elements (Call to adventure, Trials, Transformation)
- Shadow archetype manifestations

### 11. DEVELOPMENTAL INDICATORS
- Life stage awareness
- Maturity markers (psychological, emotional, spiritual)
- Growth areas they're actively working on
- Developmental challenges they're facing

### 12. PROFESSIONAL/WORK PATTERNS
- Work style preferences
- Leadership approach
- Team dynamics preferences
- Delegation and autonomy needs
- Performance drivers

### 13. FINANCIAL PSYCHOLOGY MARKERS
- Money scripts and beliefs
- Risk tolerance indicators
- Financial decision-making patterns
- Relationship with abundance/scarcity

### 14. CONTRADICTIONS & BLIND SPOTS
- Where they contradict themselves
- What they seem unaware of about themselves
- Gaps between self-perception and likely reality
- Areas of potential self-deception
